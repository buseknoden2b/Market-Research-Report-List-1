<p><p>Non-invasive intracranial pressure (ICP) monitoring devices are crucial for assessing brain health without surgical procedures. Sound signal detection utilizes acoustic methods to estimate ICP by analyzing sound wave patterns, providing real-time data. Blood flow signal detection, on the other hand, monitors variations in blood flow metrics to infer ICP changes. Both types offer clinicians safer, efficient alternatives for ICP monitoring, minimizing patient risk while enhancing decision-making capabilities in critical care settings.</p></p>
