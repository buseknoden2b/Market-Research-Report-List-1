<p><p>The internal medicine suture market comprises various types, including catgut, reconstituted collagen, polyglycolide, and others. Catgut, derived from animal intestines, is absorbable, making it ideal for internal surgeries. Reconstituted collagen sutures are biocompatible and promote healing, while polyglycolide offers a synthetic, absorbable option suitable for various procedures. Other materials may include nylon, polyester, and silk, each serving specific applications based on their tensile strength, absorbability, and tissue compatibility. These options cater to diverse surgical needs and patient requirements.</p></p>
