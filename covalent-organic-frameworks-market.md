<p><p>Covalent Organic Frameworks (COFs) are categorized into two main types based on their dimensional structures. Two-dimensional (2D) COFs consist of planar layers that form extended networks, offering high surface areas and tunable properties for applications like gas storage and separation. In contrast, three-dimensional (3D) COFs feature interconnected frameworks that enhance stability and porosity, making them suitable for various applications such as catalysis and energy storage. Both types are gaining traction in materials science due to their versatility and functionality.</p></p>
