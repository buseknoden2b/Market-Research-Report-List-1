<p><p>Material Jetting 3D printing encompasses two main market types: Polymer Jetting and Metal Jetting. Polymer Jetting involves depositing liquefied photopolymers layer by layer to create detailed, complex plastic parts with smooth finishes, making it ideal for prototypes and end-use components. Metal Jetting, on the other hand, uses metal droplets to build dense, high-strength parts, suitable for applications requiring superior mechanical properties. Both technologies offer unique advantages in customization, precision, and material diversity, catering to various industrial needs.</p></p>
