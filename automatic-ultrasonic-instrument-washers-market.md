<p><ul><li>Magnetostrictive Transducer</li><li>Piezoelectric Transducer</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Automatic ultrasonic instrument washers utilize high-frequency sound waves for cleaning, employing two main types of transducers: magnetostrictive and piezoelectric. Magnetostrictive transducers convert electrical energy into mechanical vibrations, generating ultrasonic waves through a magnetic field. In contrast, piezoelectric transducers use materials that deform under electrical voltage, producing ultrasonic frequencies. Both technologies enhance cleaning efficiency in medical, dental, and laboratory settings by effectively removing debris from intricate instruments, ensuring thorough sanitation while reducing manual labor and optimizing operational workflows.</p></p>
